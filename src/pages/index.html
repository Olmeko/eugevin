Why i don't support Internet Exlorer

Gradually, Microsoft’s standard browser is being squeezed out by other market players such as Google or Microsoft themselves with their Edge Chromium. I'm not trying to make excuses, but just show you why I stopped supporting him a long time ago:

  • Microsoft themselves are trying to destroy this ancient evil. It spoils sites, destroys markup, does not allow to use innovations
  • Internet Explorer is a crutch. And all the code that you first write for normal browsers (Edge, Chromium), then you will rewrite - and this is also a crutch
  • Internet Explorer remains in the hearts of users as a "pleasant memory." GS statistics show that less than 2% of Internet users worldwide use it
  • I pay a lot of attention to code for mobile devices, and therefore (since there is no IE on mobile devices) I do what I want and nothing will stop me
  • And in general. Animations? No, crutches. Popups? No, crutches. Performance? No, without crutches. Safety? Good luck with updating your IE6 databases once a thousand years, which certainly awaited IE8 and will wait for IE11. So why do all this?
